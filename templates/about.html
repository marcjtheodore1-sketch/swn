About Strolling with Neurokin

Our Story

Strolling with Neurokin (SwN) is an initiative run by Group Charity London, created to provide a welcoming, inclusive space for the autism community to connect, exercise, and enjoy the outdoors together.

We call them "strolls" rather than walks because we want everyone to feel welcome — whether you walk, roll, or move at your own pace. Our routes are carefully planned to be wheelchair accessible and inclusive for people with different mobility needs.

Each stroll is a 2-hour walk and talk session with a 30-minute break halfway for drinks and snacks. It's a relaxed, no-pressure environment where you can meet others who understand the autism experience.

🎉 Strolling with Neurokin is completely free. There are no fees, no charges, and no financial barriers to access. We believe everyone should have the opportunity to connect and participate.

Who Can Join?

Everyone in the wider autism community is welcome:

  • 🧠 Autistic / neurodivergent individuals
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Family members
  • 🤝 Carers
  • 💚 Allies and supporters
  • 🏢 Professionals working with autism
  • 🌈 Anyone interested in the community

Volunteer Support

🤝

Every stroll is supported by at least two London Autism Group Charity volunteers. This is a core part of how we ensure everyone's safety and comfort.

Having two volunteers present means there's always someone available to help — whether it's answering questions, providing assistance, or simply being there for a chat. Our volunteers are trained, friendly, and understand the autism community.

Alongside your walk leader, these volunteers help create a safe, welcoming environment where everyone can relax and enjoy the experience.
